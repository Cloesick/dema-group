### Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| **"I already have suppliers"** | "Of course - most successful dealers work with multiple suppliers. We're not asking you to replace anyone, just to try us for [specific category]. If we deliver, you'll naturally order more." |
| **"Your prices are too high"** | "Let's look at total cost. With 95% availability and next-day delivery, you'll win more projects and have fewer emergency runs. What's a lost project worth to you?" |
| **"I've never heard of you"** | "We've been in pumps and irrigation for [X] years. We're now building a dealer network because our customers asked for it. You'd be one of our founding partners." |
| **"I don't have time"** | "I understand - that's exactly why we built a self-service portal. Once set up, ordering takes 2 minutes. Can I show you?" |
| **"What if you compete with me?"** | "Great question. Our policy is clear: we don't sell to end users in dealer territories. It's in our agreement. Want to see it?" |

### Closing Techniques

| Technique | When to Use | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Assumptive** | Strong buying signals | "Let's get your account set up. What email should I use?" |
| **Alternative** | Need to move forward | "Would you prefer to start with Bronze tier or go straight to Silver?" |
| **Summary** | After addressing objections | "So we've agreed on pricing, delivery, and territory. What else do you need to move forward?" |
| **Urgency** | Genuine time pressure | "Our founding partner program ends this month. After that, standard terms apply." |
