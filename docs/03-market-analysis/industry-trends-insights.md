## 4. Water & Irrigation Trends

### Water Scarcity in Belgium

**Key Facts:**
- Belgium ranks **18th out of 25** countries with extreme water stress
- Uses most of renewable water supply every year
- Flanders most affected by drought
- Water systems optimized to drain water quickly (now problematic)

### Blue Deal (Flemish Government)

**Launched:** Summer 2020

**Scope:**
- 70+ ambitious actions
- Structural approach to drought and water scarcity
- Focus on supply-demand balance

**Implications for DEMA:**
- Growing demand for irrigation systems
- Water reuse solutions needed
- Efficient pumping systems valued
- Government support for water projects

### Irrigation Trends

| Current State | Future Need |
|---------------|-------------|
| Mobile reel systems | Fixed irrigation |
| Minimal irrigation | Regular irrigation |
| Basic equipment | Smart/efficient systems |
| Low investment | Significant investment |

**Opportunity:** Farmers transitioning from "no irrigation needed" to "irrigation essential" = major equipment sales opportunity.

## 5. Regulatory Environment

### PFAS Regulations

**Status:** Increasing scrutiny across Belgium

**Key Requirements:**
- Groundwater reporting limit: 10 ng/l (most PFAS)
- Drainage water quality requirements
- Existing installations deadline: March 2025
- Food chain monitoring by FASFC

**Impact on Products:**
- Non-PFAS alternatives needed
- Certification requirements
- Testing and compliance costs
- Opportunity for "PFAS-free" products

### CE Marking & Product Compliance

- All industrial products require CE marking
- Pumps: Machinery Directive, ATEX (if applicable)
- Pressure equipment: PED compliance
- Electrical: Low Voltage Directive
