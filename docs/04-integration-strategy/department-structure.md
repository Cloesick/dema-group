# DEMA Group Department Structure

## Centralized Organization Model

### Executive Summary

This document outlines the proposed departmental structure for a centralized DEMA Group, consolidating the 5 operating companies (DEMA, Fluxer, Beltz247, De Visschere Technics, Accu Components) under a unified organizational framework while preserving operational autonomy where needed.

## Department Breakdown

### 1. SHARED SERVICES (Centralized)

**Purpose:** Eliminate duplication, achieve economies of scale, standardize processes

#### 1.1 Finance & Administration
| Function | Current State | Centralized Model |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Accounting | 5 separate | 1 team, 5 cost centers |
| Invoicing | 5 systems | 1 unified system |
| Payroll | 5 processes | 1 centralized |
| Credit control | 5 separate | 1 team |
| Reporting | Fragmented | Consolidated dashboard |

**Headcount:** 3-4 FTE (vs. estimated 6-8 currently across group)
**Savings:** €50-80K/year

#### 1.2 Human Resources
| Function | Current State | Centralized Model |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Recruitment | Ad-hoc | Centralized talent acquisition |
| Training | Separate | Group training programs |
| Payroll admin | Separate | Centralized |
| Performance mgmt | Inconsistent | Unified framework |

**Headcount:** 1-2 FTE
**Savings:** €20-30K/year

#### 1.3 IT & Digital
| Function | Current State | Centralized Model |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Websites | 5 separate | 1 unified platform |
| ERP/Inventory | Separate systems | Integrated ERP |
| E-commerce | Only Accu has | Unified webshop |
| Support | Ad-hoc | Centralized helpdesk |
| Development | None | In-house/outsourced team |

**Headcount:** 2-3 FTE + external partners
**Investment:** €300K Year 1, €100K/year ongoing
**Savings:** €50-100K/year (after Year 2)

#### 1.4 Marketing & Communications
| Function | Current State | Centralized Model |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Branding | 5 separate brands | Unified "DEMA Group" umbrella |
| Website content | 5 separate | 1 content team |
| Social media | Minimal | Coordinated presence |
| Trade shows | Separate booths | Joint presence |
| Catalogs | Separate | Unified digital catalog |

**Headcount:** 1-2 FTE
**Savings:** €30-50K/year

### 3. OPERATIONS DEPARTMENT (Centralized Coordination)

**Purpose:** Unified logistics, inventory optimization, purchasing power

#### 3.1 Purchasing & Procurement
| Function | Current State | Centralized Model |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Supplier negotiations | 5 separate | Consolidated purchasing |
| Order management | Separate | Unified PO system |
| Supplier relationships | Fragmented | Strategic partnerships |

**Savings from consolidated purchasing:** €150-300K/year (5-10% on €3M+ spend)

#### 3.2 Warehouse & Logistics
| Function | Current State | Centralized Model |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Warehouses | 3+ locations | Consolidated + satellites |
| Inventory | Separate stock | Shared safety stock |
| Delivery | Separate routes | Optimized routing |
| Picking/Packing | Separate | Centralized for overlap |

**Options:**
- **Option A:** Single consolidated warehouse (Roeselare or Lichtervelde)
- **Option B:** Hub-and-spoke (main warehouse + 2 satellites)
- **Option C:** Virtual consolidation (shared inventory visibility, separate physical)

**Recommended:** Option B for balance of efficiency and customer proximity

### 5. BUSINESS UNITS (Operational Autonomy)

**Purpose:** Maintain market focus, customer relationships, specialized expertise

#### 5.1 Business Unit Structure

Each original company becomes a Business Unit with:
- **P&L responsibility** (revenue targets, cost control)
- **Customer relationships** (existing accounts)
- **Product expertise** (category management)
- **Operational execution** (day-to-day)

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐

│   (Pumps,   │  (Valves,   │ (Conveyor   │ (Irrigation │ (Precision  │
